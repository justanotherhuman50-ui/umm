01 About Me

Maker at heart.
Engineer by discipline.

It started with a Bluetooth robotic arm at 13, built before I knew what a datasheet was, or that datasheets existed. What I did know was that I wanted to understand why things worked, not just that they did. That curiosity has been compounding ever since.

Over the years I've gone progressively lower in the stack. From edge ML at ShrimpIQ, to designing a free-space optical data link from scratch at NIT Rourkela's CommNet Lab, to writing bare-metal firmware on ARM Cortex cores, building HLS pipelines on Zynq FPGAs, and flying a drone on a control stack I wrote myself. Each project taught me the layer I didn't know I was missing.

What I actually enjoy is the moment a system clicks. When you understand it deeply enough to optimise it, not just use it. That's what keeps me going past the comfortable abstraction and into the register map.
